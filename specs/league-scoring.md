Every league has a scoring system that calculates points earned by each player and team during a round and matchup.

The scoring is based on handicap-adjusted scores (net score), and points are awarded for each hole as well as a team total at the end of the round.

## Teams

Every league is split into many teams of 2 players each. For example, it is common to have 20 teams of 2 in a league for a total of 40 golfers.

Each member of a team has their own handicap, and they can vary wildly. For example, an excellent player with a low handicap of 1 may be paired on a team with a newer golfer who has a high handicap, like 17. Alternatively, two good players with a similar handicap of 4 and 5 could be together on a team:

- First team
  - AJ Greenman (1 Handicap)
  - Gary Greenman (17 Handicap)
- Second team
  - Brady Greenman (4 Handicap)
  - Aaron Greenman (5 Handicap)

During a match between two teams, the lower handicap players on each team are paired in a 1v1 match and are considered the "A" players. So in our scenario, the player with a handicap of 1 from the first team would go head to head against the player with a handicap of 4 from the second team. These two would be considered the A players for their teams.

The player with the higher handicap on each team would then be considered the "B" player, and would play the opposing "B" player. So in our example, the first team's B player would be the 17 handicap, and the second team's B player would be the 5 handicap.

## Scoring by hole

For each hole during a round, two points are up for grabs for each individual matchup, as well as an additional point for each team matchup.

If player A beats the opposing player A in net (handicap adjusted) score, they are awarded the full two points. On the other hand, if the opposing player wins, they earn the full two points. A point is awarded to each side if they split the hole (tie).

The same goes for the B players, so individually 4 points can be awarded per hole.

Then, an additional point can be earned via the total of the team's net score on that hole. So for that, you'd add up the combined net scores of each player on each team, and the lower of the two would get an additional point.

For a typical 9 hole round, this means 45 points are available to be earned during each matchup.

### Example

Here is an example of 1 hole's points:

Team 1      | Hole 2 Gross  | Hole 2 Net    | Points
Player A    | 4             | 4             | 2.0
Player B    | 6             | 5             | 1.0
Total       | 10            | 9             | 1.0

Team 2      | Hole 2 Gross  | Hole 2 Net    | Points
Player A    | 5             | 5             | 0.0
Player B    | 5             | 5             | 1.0
Total       | 10            | 10            | 0.0

## Scoring by round

Finally, an additional 5 points are awarded in the same matter as per hole, except this is for the entire round's total score. So again, you'd compare the A players total scores, and then the B players total scores, and then the teams as a whole's total scores (all net or handicap adjusted, of course).

This means for every matchup, 50 points are possible (45 points from the 9 holes, plus 5 from the total).
